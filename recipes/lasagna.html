Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types, made of very wide, flat sheets. Either term can also refer to an Italian dish made of stacked layers of lasagna alternating with fillings such as rag√π (ground meats and tomato sauce), vegetables, cheeses (which may include ricotta, mozzarella, and parmesan), and seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Egg
  • Tomato sauce
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add lasagna noodles and cook for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente; drain.
  3. In a medium bowl, combine ricotta, 2 cups mozzarella cheese, and eggs.
  4. In a 9x13 inch baking dish, combine tomato sauce, garlic, onion, and salt.
  5. Arrange 4 noodles lengthwise in the bottom of the pan. Cover with 1 cup ricotta mixture, 1 cup mozzarella cheese, and 1 cup Parmesan cheese. Repeat layers twice more.
  6. Bake at 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) for 30 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly.
